# Actor 13 — United Kingdom
## Bloc 4 — High Ambition Coalition

> **Keep this document private.** Your mandate describes who you are and what you want from the agreement. You may tell other groups what you want — you may not show them this document.

**Who you are:** You hosted COP26 in Glasgow. Your grid is now over 50% renewable. But domestic policy backsliding — delayed petrol car bans, new North Sea oil licences — has undermined your moral authority. The gap between your pledges and your actions is visible. Name it.

**What you want:**
- Countries that pledged stronger climate action at COP26 must show they are actually delivering — new pledges before old ones are met is not credible
- Specific targets for deploying clean technology sector by sector — power, transport, industry — rather than vague economy-wide commitments
- Using public money to attract private investment in clean energy (blended finance), but acknowledge this approach has limits in the poorest countries where private capital does not flow
- Your domestic backsliding — new North Sea oil licences, delayed petrol car bans — is your vulnerability
- **Red line:** Any weakening of the 1.5°C target
- **Minimum condition:** A binding mechanism to track whether countries are actually delivering on their pledges, with public reporting

**Your welfare lens:** Your mandate calls for a framework that **maximises total welfare across all regions**. Under this framework, UK clean technology investment generates global returns. But know that under a framework that weights the worst-off, the pledge-delivery gap translates into concrete harm for the most exposed.

*(Regions likely relevant to your case: `gbr`.)*

**Some analytical directions to consider:**
1. Does the `gbr` emissions trajectory in your preferred Pareto solution match your stated commitments? If not, what does the gap imply?
2. What does `fraction_of_ensemble_above_threshold` look like at 1.5°C and 2°C under your preferred solution? How do you argue credibly for 1.5°C when the model's best achievable outcome may fall short of it?
3. Under a framework that gives extra weight to the worst-off, what does the UK's delivery gap imply for `rjan57` and `rsaf`? Run this before the debate.
